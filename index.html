Fitness Web App Project Proposal

Provide an overview of the general topic or question of interest (e.g., "what is meant by 'homelessness' and why is it a particular problem in Seattle?").

The topic that we are trying to tackle is fitness by creating an application that tailors a fitness program toward the user's goals. So if the user is interested in hypertrophy, we will create a fitness regime based on building muscle. If the user is interested in powerlifting, we will create a regime around increasing the user's max numbers. We are creating this application because a lot of people in fitness experience paralysis by analysis because there are so many different workout, splits; so, through our application, we are able to solve this for the user by focusing on their goals.

An information technology problem that a fitness app that provides a workout plan, diet, and rest guidance could face is a lack of personalization. Many fitness apps offer generic workout plans and diets that may not consider an individual's unique fitness goals, physical limitations, or dietary restrictions. Additionally, the app may not be able to adjust the workout plan or diet as the user progresses and reaches new fitness milestones. This issue could lead to frustration for users who are not seeing the desired results and may cause them to stop using the app altogether. Another issue could be the lack of integration with other health-related apps and devices, such as heart rate monitors, sleep trackers, and nutrition apps, which would provide more comprehensive data and personalized recommendations to the user.

Our Vision

A common area of interest in our group is fitness, and we are looking to build a web app that contains various different fitness programs. We believe our web app could be beneficial to a range of different people, from those just looking to start going to the gym to people that are more advanced in their fitness journey.

We aim to provide helpful fitness information tailored to each individual by creating an interface for the user to find different fitness programs based on their goals. For example, a user looking to lose weight could select fat loss as one of the goals when searching for a fitness program. After selecting a program, our web app would list out the various exercises in the program. By clicking on each exercise, there would be directions on how to perform the exercise with visual aids correctly. Through such a system, we could recommend appropriate programs to a wide range of users.

Besides offering fitness programs, we also want to cater to users who simply wish to quickly search through different exercises. For example, if a bodybuilder is searching for a new chest exercise, by selecting chest exercise, our web app will recommend different chest exercises, and our web app will offer the same functionalities as previously described when the user clicks on the various exercises.
